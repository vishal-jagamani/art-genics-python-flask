Monalisa (1503-1519)

Mona Lisa, also known as La Gioconda, is the wife of Francesco del Giocondo. This painting is painted as oil on wood. The original painting size is 77 x 53 cm (30 x 20 7/8 in) and is owned by the Government of France and is on the wall in the Louvre in Paris, France. This figure of a woman, dressed in the Florentine fashion of her day and seated in a visionary, mountainous landscape. The Mona Lisa’s enigmatic expression, which seems both alluring and aloof, has given the portrait universal fame.

Artist: Leonardo da Vinci

The Starry Night (1889)

The Starry Night is an oil on canvas by Dutch post-impressionist painter Vincent van Gogh. Painted in June 1889, it describes the view from the east-facing window of his asylum room at Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, just before sunrise, with the addition of an ideal village.

Artist: Vincent van Gogh

Location: The Museum of Modern Art
